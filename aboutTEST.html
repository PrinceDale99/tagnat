Welcome to Taguig National High School

Welcome to Taguig National High School (TNHS), a place of excellence in education. We take pride in our rich history, dedicated faculty, and diverse student community.

Our journey began on January 8, 1980, as Fort Bonifacio College Annex and evolved into Taguig Municipal High School in 1982. Finally, we became Taguig National High School in 1994, serving as a government public institution committed to providing quality education.

With a vast campus located at Imelda Romualdez Marcos Road and in the vibrant neighborhoods of Barangay Maharlika Village and Barangay New Lower Bicutan, TNHS is at the heart of Taguig City's educational landscape.

Our commitment is to empower students with knowledge, skills, and values that will prepare them for a bright future.

Our Diverse Student Community

At TNHS, we celebrate diversity in our student body. Our students come from various backgrounds, with different languages and cultures. You'll find a harmonious blend of Roman Catholic, Muslim, and other religious backgrounds, contributing to a rich cultural tapestry within our school.

We encourage language diversity too. While Filipino (Tagalog) is the primary language of communication, our students also engage in English for academic excellence. Furthermore, Arabic is used in Madrasa classes, and students even have the opportunity to explore languages like French and Portuguese, offering a truly multicultural experience.

At TNHS, we believe that this diversity is our strength, and it prepares our students for a globalized world.
